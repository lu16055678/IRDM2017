Getting to UCL

If you are coming from abroad or have not yet visited the main UCL Campus, the address you need to head to is Gower Street, London, WC1E 6BT.  UCL is in an area of London known as Bloomsbury.

Detailed instructions about reaching UCL by public transport are on the  UCL map web pages. The nearest mainline train station is Euston, and the nearest underground stations are Goodge Street and Euston Square.  You can also download the UCLGO! App to access maps and other useful UCL University and Union information on the go.

The Transport for London Journey Planner (link below) is useful for making travel arrangements.

Finding the Computer Science Department

The Department of Computer Science is based in the Malet Place Engineering Building, ( MPEB), at the southern end of campus. Instructions for reaching us are at www.cs.ucl.ac.uk/about_us/getting_here/

Finding your way to a specific room

Rooms at UCL are usually represented by a room code with the exception of some lecture theatres and rooms that are also named after people.  The room code consists of the building name, a floor number, a dot, then the room number.  For example MPEB 1.02 = Malet Place Engineering Building, 1st Floor, Room 2.

UCL is a big place and so you will be given a map as part of your induction pack to help you find your way around in the first few weeks. 

Useful Tips

UCL also has a routefinder system which is very useful for finding your way to a particular place, or if you have an iPhone or iPod Touch you can download the UCL iMap app (developed by a UCL CS student), to help you navigate your way around campus.
